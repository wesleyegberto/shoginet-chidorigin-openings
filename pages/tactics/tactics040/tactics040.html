Double Side Pawn Picker (Ai-yoko-fudori) [2]

Currently, the Ai-yoko-fudori (Double Side Pawn Picker) is rarely played among professionals because many variations are considered promising for Sente. However, a definitive conclusion has not yet been reached, and there are many amateurs who specialize in this style. Studying the joseki to handle its various branches is a wonderful way to enjoy the glamour and excitement of Shogi. That said, for those looking for a simpler countermeasure, I would like to introduce a way of playing that thwarts the opponent's objective and leads to a more gradual development.

When Sente plays ▲７七銀 and Gote challenges with a Rook exchange via ▽７四飛, Sente can quickly pull back with ▲３六飛 to avoid the trade. This completely thwarts Gote's intention of forcing a violent development through a Rook exchange. While it might seem like a passive move, avoiding your opponent’s specialty is a perfectly valid strategy. Furthermore, Gote’s subsequent moves from Figure 2 are surprisingly difficult.

Sente is now threatening ▲２二歩, which Gote must defend against. However, ▽２四飛 fails to ▲１五角 (a quasi-oute-bishatori, or Forking the King and Rook), and if Gote develops with ▽３三桂, Sente plays ▲６六角. Following ▽２四飛 ▲３三角成 ▽同金 ▲同飛成 ▽２九飛成 ▲３一龍, Sente wins.

The sequence up to Figure 3 is just one example of how the game might progress from Figure 2. In Figure 2, since both players have captured each other's side pawns, there is no material difference. The distinction lies in the state of Sente's 7th and 8th files versus Gote's 2nd and 3rd files. As you can see, Sente's pieces are more developed. The key to maintaining an advantage is for Sente to play in a way that capitalizes on this superior development.

After ▽３三歩, Sente plays ▲２六飛 because Gote is threatening ▽２四飛. Gote's ▽４四角 is a move that cannot be taken lightly; if Sente retreats with ▲２八飛, they fall into a losing position after ▽２七歩 ▲同飛 ▽２六歩 ▲２八飛 ▽７七角成 ▲同金 ▽２七銀.

The ▲４八銀 and ▲３八金 formation is considered one of the ideal shapes in Yoko-fudori patterns. By the time Figure 3 is reached, Sente's strategy after defending with ▲８六歩 becomes clear: with a Bishop in hand and a superior number of Pawns, Sente has achieved a strategic victory (sakusen-gachi).
　

The joseki for Ai-yoko-fudori (Double Side Pawn Picker) contains extremely interesting variations, and studying them will certainly lead to an improvement in your overall Shogi strength. For those interested, I encourage you to look into specialized joseki books.

However, from a competitive standpoint, thwarting your opponent's intentions is also an effective strategy. Since opponents who initiate this opening often do not have developments like the one in Figure 2 within their range of preparation, you may find that this approach works surprisingly well.
