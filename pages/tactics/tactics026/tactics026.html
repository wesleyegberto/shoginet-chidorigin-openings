If Sente plays ▲９七角 in "Figure 1" as in the previous chapter, Gote will sacrifice a pawn with ▽９五歩 and then promote the Rook with ▽８九飛成. When Sente retreats with ▲８八角, Gote follows up with the troublesome pawn drop ▽９八歩. Because Gote's ▽７二銀 formation prevents Sente's ▲８一飛成, the ▲８六飛 maneuver loses its effectiveness. Sente is forced to challenge the Rook with ▲８六飛, but as shown in "Figure 2," Gote gains a Knight and takes a superior position. If Gote had played ▽６二銀, Sente could ignore the threat to the Bishop and counterattack strongly with ▲８三飛; however, in this case, Gote’s formation has no gaps, leaving Sente with no choice but to take with ▲同桂. Consequently, playing ▲９七角 against this specific lineup is considered an unreasonable line of play.

When Gote adopts the ▽７二銀 formation, playing the pawn drop ▲８五歩 has become the standard theory (joseki). While one would prefer to force Gote to be the only one spending a pawn on defense without using one's own, in this case, it is unavoidable. To bolster the offensive, Sente brings the Silver up to ▲５六銀 and initiates the attack with ▲６五歩.

If Gote takes this with ▽同歩, Sente can employ the attack starting with the ▲６四歩 drop introduced in "Figure 3" of the previous chapter. Therefore, Gote reinforces the 6th file with ▽５三銀. However, as the sequence progresses to "Figure 3," Gote becomes unable to defend the 2nd file, resulting in a winning advantage for Sente. During this sequence, if Gote tries to take ▲３四銀 with ▽同金, Sente responds with the devastating ▲５三角成. In this way, Tatefudori is an excellent strategy that offers offensive lines from both the left and right sides.

Because the ▽３三金 defensive formation often leads to a disadvantageous opening (sakusen-make), strategies have emerged where Gote avoids Tatefudori altogether by delaying the ▽３四歩 pawn push in the early game. In response, Sente must apply further ingenuity, which we will explore in the next chapter.
