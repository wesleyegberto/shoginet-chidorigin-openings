If Gote responds to Sente's ▲２四同飛 by pushing ▽８六歩, the progression to Figure 1 is inevitable. From here, if Sente takes the side pawn with ▲３四飛, it marks the opening of a brilliant and intense battle. However, there is a way for Sente to launch a rapid attack from this very position.

In Figure 1, the sequence starting with ▲２二角成 followed by the ▲７七角 drop exists. At first glance, this appears to be a win for Sente. However, after ▲２二飛成 (a two-piece exchange of a Rook for a Gold and Silver), Gote has the counter of ▽７九龍. If Sente takes with ▲同金, Gote plays the ▽７七角 drop—a fork on the King and Rook—resulting in a win for Gote.

Even if Sente does not take the ▽７九龍 and instead defends with ▲６九金, the following sequence—▽９九龍, ▲２一龍, ▽６二玉—leaves Sente at a significant material disadvantage and in a losing position, as shown in Figure 3.
Figure 3

We have now seen that playing ▲２二角成, ▽同銀, and then dropping ▲７七角 from Figure 1 is an unsound line of attack. However, I would like you to remember this sequence and tactical theme. This specific attacking pattern appears in other opening styles as well, and depending on the formation, there are times when this offensive maneuver actually succeeds.
