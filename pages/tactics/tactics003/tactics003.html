Bishop Exchange (Kakugawari)

Among Static Rook (Ibisha) games that start with Sente opening the Bishop's diagonal with ▲7六歩, there is a strategy where both players exchange Bishops early in the opening and proceed with the game while holding a Bishop in hand. This tactical formation is called Kakugawari (Bishop Exchange).

When Sente responds to Gote's ▽8四歩 by pushing the Rook-file pawn with ▲2六歩, the game takes a different path from the previously mentioned Yagura and often leads to this Kakugawari (Bishop Exchange). As you can see, while there are exceptions, the right to choose the strategy basically lies with Sente (the first player).

In the case of Kakugawari, there are different move orders to reach this point. Furthermore, on Gote's 10th move, they could avoid the exchange by playing ▽4四歩 instead of ▽7七角成. Therefore, this strategy is typically established only when both players agree to it.

From the diagram above, a common progression would be ▽4二銀, ▲3八銀, and ▽7二銀. However, many other variations exist, and since this sequence can branch into various complex patterns, it is considered a somewhat advanced and difficult strategy to master.
