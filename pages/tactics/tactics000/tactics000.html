Since the "Wakaba-tei" is a meeting room within Shogi Chess Net specifically intended for beginners, I would like to start a series called "Tactics Illustrated" (Senpou Zukan). Although it is a humble effort, I hope this introduction to simple maneuvers will be helpful to those just starting out.

While many guidebooks explain various strategies, the variations are often so diverse and complex that beginners may find it difficult to fully grasp them while reading. Therefore, in this series, I will minimize complex variations and instead focus on introducing specific scenarios where the core objective of a tactic is perfectly executed. In martial arts like judo, to learn a technique, one must first see the "Ippon"—the form of the technique perfectly succeeding—to understand what it is all about.

My goal is to provide a "roadmap" for those who have truly just started learning Shogi on how to actually move their pieces from the opening. In that sense, I have titled this 【Tactics Illustrated】. I will keep the explanations as simple as possible, so please feel free to ask questions if you have any. If you find a tactic you want to understand more deeply, this series can also serve as a guide for choosing professional opening books (joseki books).

Now, let us begin with the very first move of a Shogi game. The game starts with Sente (Black) making the first move. So, what is that first move?

With few exceptions, there are two moves considered the "best" for the opening. Please look at the diagram below. This shows the state after both Sente and Gote have made one move each.


The Rook and Bishop are the most powerful major pieces in Shogi, but at the start of the game, the Bishop is unable to move. Furthermore, the Rook—your opponent's other major piece—is directly staring at the "head" of your Bishop, which is its primary weakness.

Therefore, the moves that activate these two major pieces are considered the best first moves: for Sente, these are ▲7六歩 (opening the Bishop's diagonal) and ▲2六歩 (advancing the Rook's pawn). For Gote, the corresponding moves are ▽8四歩 and ▽3四歩. The combination of these opening moves determines how the game's strategy will branch out in the early stages.
