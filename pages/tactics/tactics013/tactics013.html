Fourth-File Rook – Piece Development

As an opening where the Rook is moved horizontally, we previously introduced the "Primitive Central Rook" in Chapter 8. However, in authentic Furibisha (Ranging Rook) strategies, piece development proceeds as shown in the opening patterns from Chapter 5.
Among these, the most widely played is the Shiken-bisha (Fourth-file Rook) strategy—where the Rook is moved to the fourth file from the left—which was used as an example in Chapter 6. I will explain this strategy, like I did with Yagura, divided into a "Development Phase" and a "Combat Phase."

Since Ranging Rook is often employed by the Gote, I will explain it here with Gote playing the Fourth-file Rook. It is, of course, easily possible to use this same setup when playing as Sente.

There are many strategies the Static Rook side can use against Ranging Rook, but Sente's formation at this point is called the Funa-gakoi (Boat Castle). By pushing the ▲3六歩 pawn, Sente is likely signaling an intention to employ Kyusen (Rapid Attack) tactics.

Regarding the edge pawns, depending on the strategy, it is sometimes better not to respond. However, unlike in Yagura, responding to an edge pawn push in the opening here is not necessarily a bad move. Especially in a position like this where a rapid attack seems likely, it is generally advantageous to respond when the opponent pushes the edge pawn.

Sente's formation in Figure 2 is known exactly as the 5七銀左 type (Go-nana-gin-hidari, Left-57 Silver Formation). It is the most popular rapid-attack formation used by Static Rook against Ranging Rook. There is also a variation where the right Silver advances to 5七. On the other side, Gote's King is protected by the "Mino-gakoi" (Mino Castle), which was introduced in Chapter 9.

Gote's move of ▽4三銀 in response to Sente's ▲5七銀左 is intended to reinforce the defense of the Bishop's head. This prepares for Sente's "Naname Bougin" (Diagonal Climbing Silver) attack, which starts with ▲3五歩 (if ▽同歩, then ▲4六銀). Unlike the standard Climbing Silver, this strategy is named "Diagonal" because the Silver advances diagonally toward the 2四 square.

At this stage, you can probably see why a Ranging Rook player closes the Bishop's path in the early opening: it is specifically to avoid early Bishop exchanges while setting up this defensive structure.
