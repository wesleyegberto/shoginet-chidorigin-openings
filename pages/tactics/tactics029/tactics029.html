If Gote does not defend the edge and instead plays ▽3四歩 in response to ▲1六歩, moving the Rook to ▲3六飛 to aim for a Tatefudori (Vertical Pawn Take) strategy would be a loss, as it would make ▲1六歩 a completely wasted move. It is more advantageous to push further with ▲1五歩, respond to Gote's Rook-file exchange with a standard ▲8七歩 drop, and aim for a different tactical formation rather than pursuing Hineri-bisha.

Sente quickly organizes an offensive stance by utilizing the Knight with ▲3六歩 followed by ▲3七桂. Gote counters by moving ▽4二玉 and advancing the Silver to ▽5四銀. This formation, where the Silver sits atop the 5th-file Pawn, is called "Koshikake-gin" (Reclining Silver).

Choosing between ▲5八玉 and ▲6八玉 is a difficult decision, but if aiming for an ultra-rapid attack, ▲5八玉 seems preferable as it prevents the King from being placed in immediate check by the Bishop from any diagonal. Gote tightens their defense with ▽5二金, leading to "Figure 2."

First, Sente sacrifices a pawn with ▲3五歩, followed by ▲1四歩, ▽同歩, a ▲1三歩 drop, and ▽同香, finally jumping the Knight with ▲2五桂. This is the rapid-attack method for this formation. If Gote had defended with ▽1四歩 previously, the exchange would have resulted in an ▲1五歩 / ▽同歩 shape; in that case, Gote could escape with ▽1四香 when the Knight jumps to 2-5, making this attack a "bad line" (unreasonable).

The sequence provided is just one example, and by "Figure 3," Sente has secured the win. In response to the final move, the ▲3四歩 drop: if Gote plays ▽同玉, Sente counters with ▲4六桂; if the King retreats downward, Sente drops ▲3三銀; and if the King escapes to ▽4四玉, Sente plays ▲2三飛成. By following up with standard pursuit moves, all variations lead to a Yorisuji (a formation where the King can be cornered and checkmated).

Of course, many other variations exist, but the offensive line starting from ▲3五歩 is surprisingly difficult to defend against. In a real game, the attacking side likely has the advantage and a higher probability of winning.

Incidentally, reaching "Figure 3" clarifies why the King was moved to ▲5八玉. If the King had remained in its starting position (Igyoku), Gote would have been able to play ▽1五角 during the sequence—a powerful move that simultaneously targets the King and the Rook (Jun-Oute-Hisha-Tori).

For the reasons stated above, it is considered safer for Gote to respond to Sente's ▲1六歩 with ▽1四歩 in the Aigakari (Double Wing Attack) opening. Please commit the ▲3五歩 pawn sacrifice (Tsukisute) to memory, as it is an extremely important tactical maneuver (tesuji).
