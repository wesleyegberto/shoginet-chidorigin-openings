By moving ▽5四銀, Gote prevents the advance of ▲4五歩. When Sente reinforces their attacking force with ▲3七桂, Gote advances the Silver to ▽6五銀. This tactic is called "Gyokuto-gin" (King's Head Silver) and is a method sometimes adopted by the Ranging Rook side. Following this, Sente attempts to force a Bishop exchange as planned, but Gote counterattacks starting with ▽8七銀成 and pivots the Rook to the 8th file. By "Figure 3," Gote has reached a decisively winning position.

Including the previous chapter, the Ranging Rook style of play is less about "attacking" in a straightforward way and more about exchanging pieces efficiently or moving them to squares where they can function most effectively. This is called "Sabaku" (clearing/handling pieces). Due to this nature of "Sabaku," the Ranging Rook formation rarely resembles the ideal attacking structure of the 3 Golden Rules (using the Rook, Bishop, Silver, and Knight) in the early game. Conversely, if the Knight and Lance on the Rook's side can be successfully utilized ("cleared"), it often leads to a superior game.
