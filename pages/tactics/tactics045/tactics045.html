Against Gote's aim to utilize the Knight with ▽７四歩 followed by ▽７三桂, Sente also attempts to develop their own Knight with ▲３六歩. Here, Gote drops ▽２五歩, utilizing the horizontal range of the Rook at ８五; after ▲２八飛, Gote proceeds to take the side pawn with the sequence ▽８六歩, ▲同歩, and ▽同飛. Following this, Gote maneuvers the Rook dynamically, and by Figure 2, the exchange of Rooks becomes inevitable with ▽２五飛, leading to an advantage for Gote. In the middle of the sequence, if Sente plays ▲同飛 against ▽２六歩, Gote has the counter ▽４四角. An exchange of Rooks at this stage clearly favors Gote, as Sente's left wing is cramped (a "wall" formation), while Gote's camp is sturdy and resilient against Rook drops.

This is an example of what happens when Sente plays ▲６八玉; Gote completes their formation with ▽７四歩 followed by ▽７三桂, then stations the Rook in the center with ▽５五飛, aiming at Sente's 5th file. The sequence shown is a variation following Sente taking with ▲同角, which leaves Sente with the threat of a ▽４四角 drop and makes the 7th file difficult to defend. In this position, Gote has the advantage.

In both of the examples above, Sente's questionable moves led to a favorable result for Gote. However, there are naturally many variations for both ▲５八玉 and ▲６八玉, and Sente has other alternative methods; the strategy continues to evolve even now. To be honest, it is a complex strategy for beginners, but since it appears frequently in televised matches and other settings, I decided to introduce it briefly, as even a little knowledge of it is certainly worthwhile.
