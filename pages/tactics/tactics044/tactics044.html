Side Pawn Picker - ▽8五 Rook Strategy [1]

Currently in professional matches, one variation of the Side Pawn Picker (Yokofudori) frequently played on the big stages of title matches is the ▽8五 Rook strategy. This formation is also known as the "Nakaza Rook" because it was the original invention of Makoto Nakaza, 5-dan.

After playing ▲３六飛 and seeing Gote move ▽２二銀, Sente plays ▲８七歩, to which Gote responds by pulling the Rook back with ▽８五飛. At the time, it was thought that such an unstable move could not possibly be good. Even professionals felt this way; in fact, during the post-game review (kanso-sen) of the very first game this was played in, the opponent remarked that they "thought Gote had simply misclicked the square to retreat the Rook."

However, this strange Rook retreat would go on to become an incredibly effective strategy, leaving behind a record unprecedented in the history of Shogi: a winning percentage for Gote exceeding 60%.

Since ▽２四飛 is a threat, Sente plays ▲２六飛, followed by ▽４一玉. Compared to the Nakahara style from the previous chapter, the only difference is whether the Rook is on ８四 or ８五. Here, there is a line where Sente exchanges Bishops and drops ▲９六角. During this sequence, taking ▲６五歩 with ▽同飛 is a loss for Gote, as Sente responds with ▲７七桂, ▽６四飛, and ▲６五歩, resulting in Sente simply developing their Knight for free.

In Figure 2, Sente has created a Horse with a check, making it appear as though Sente has the advantage.

When Gote defends with ▽５二金, there are several positions where Sente can retreat their Horse, but no matter where it goes, the drop of ▽４四角 remains an option. The reason Gote did not take the ▲６五歩 on the 9th move was to ensure this move would result in a fork of the Rook and Lance. If Sente had played ▲７七桂, the ▽４四角 drop would not exist. By playing ▲２四飛, Sente tries to prevent the fork by aiming for ▲８四飛 if Gote plays ▽９九角成, but Gote forces an exchange of the Rook and Bishop with ▽２三銀, after which the situation becomes easier for Gote to play.

At the point of ▽２三銀, there is also the option of checking with ▽７七角成 and then snatching the Rook with ▽２四飛. However, if the Horse has retreated to the right side, it is better strategy to follow the sequence until Figure 3, trapping the Horse and targeting the left wing. If the Horse retreats to ▲９六馬 in response to ▽５二金, then playing ▽７七角成 at the time of ▲２四飛 becomes the better move. The key point is to avoid engaging Sente's Horse directly. I have briefly explained the variation where Sente exchanges Bishops and drops ▲９六角 to target the Rook at ８五, but while other variations exist, none of them lead to very desirable results for Sente.
