Double Wing Attack - Vertical Pawn Take (Tatefudori) [3]

The move ▲３六飛, where Sente moves to capture the ▽３四歩 that Gote advanced to open the Bishop's diagonal, may seem like a meaningless maneuver at first glance. However, it leads to an unexpected development. Within the sequence of the Tatefudori (Vertical Pawn Take) strategy, the move that surprises everyone when they see it for the first time is ▲９七角 from "Figure 3" in the previous chapter.

"Figure 1" is the same position as "Figure 3" from the previous chapter. For the sequence of moves up to this point, please refer to Chapters 23 and 24. In this position, the move ▽８九飛成 to promote the Rook should naturally be apparent. Let’s look at what happens if Gote proceeds to promote the Rook.

When Sente responds to ▽８九飛成 by pulling the Bishop back with ▲８八角, Gote's Dragon is rendered immobile. Since the subsequent maneuver of ▲８六飛 would make the promotion at ▲８一飛成 unavoidable, Gote plays ▽７二金 and meets ▲８六飛 with ▽８三歩. However, after "killing" the Dragon and resulting in an exchange of the Rook for the Bishop, Sente reaches a superior position as seen in "Figure 2."

In this sequence, ▲５九金 is a vital move. If this is neglected, by the time "Figure 2" is reached, Gote can play ▽７九角—a fork targeting both the Rook and Gold. This forces ▲８九銀, leading to ▽８八角成 and ▲同銀, which allows Gote to reclaim the Rook. Thus, baiting the Rook promotion was actually a trap set by the Tatefudori side.

If Gote notices the trap and chooses not to promote the Rook, Sente proceeds as planned by playing ▲８六飛, challenging Gote's Rook directly. This is the true objective of the Tatefudori strategy. Because the Rook is maneuvered in a twisting motion—from 2-4 to 2-6 and then to 8-6—it is most commonly referred to as Hineri-bisha (Twisting Rook). However, since there are versions of Hineri-bisha that move the Rook to the left side without the intermediate ▲３六飛 step, I am explaining this specific variation as Tatefudori. Gote still finds a Rook exchange disadvantageous here and must defend; however, playing ▽８五歩 allows Sente to simply take with ▲同飛, so Gote has no choice but to play ▽８四歩.

With the King moved to 3-9 and the Rook swung to the 7th file via ▲７六飛, Sente’s formation resembles the Ishida Style, with the key differences being that Sente holds two Pawns in hand and has already exchanged the Pawns on the 8th and 2nd files. In response, Gote’s ▽６三銀 and ▽７二金 are standard defensive maneuvers for this formation. The following is one example of an attack using those held Pawns.

If Gote takes ▲６四歩 with ▽同銀, the sequence leading to "Figure 3" results in a winning advantage for Sente. Therefore, Gote will likely dodge with ▽５四銀, leading to a game where Sente exchanges Pawns on the 7th file with ▲７四歩, ▽同歩, and ▲同飛. In any case, it is not an enjoyable prospect for Gote to let Sente play so freely. Consequently, Gote will look to improve their formation.
