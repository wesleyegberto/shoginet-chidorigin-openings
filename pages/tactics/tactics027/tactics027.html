Double Wing Attack - Twisting Rook (Hineri-bisha) Strategy [1]

To avoid Sente's ▲３六飛 in the Tatefudori opening, Gote may choose to delay opening the Bishop's diagonal with ▽３四歩. In such cases, Sente can still play ▲３六飛 to lie in wait; this formation is known as the "Cat Style Tatefudori" (Nekoshiki Tatefudori) because Sente looks like a cat poised to pounce on its prey (▽３四歩).
However, in this chapter, we will look at a strategy where Sente also omits ▲３六飛 to counter Gote’s refusal to open the diagonal. Since this is no longer a "Vertical Pawn Take," we will explain it as the "Hineri-bisha" (Twisting Rook) strategy.

Following Gote's 12th move, ▽７二銀, Sente pushes the edge pawn with ▲１六歩. In the previous chapters, we saw Sente play ▲３八銀 instead. In fact, ▲１六歩 is currently considered the best move. To help you understand why this is the optimal choice, I thought it best to look at the historical progression in order, which is why the previous chapter explained the older sequence using ▲３八銀.

If Sente plays ▲３八銀 instead of ▲１六歩, Gote considers ▽３四歩 unfavorable and has started to play ▽６四歩 while keeping the Bishop's diagonal closed. At this point, Sente could attempt the "Cross-Rook" (Juuji-bisha) tactic by dropping a pawn at ▲２四歩, followed by ▽同歩 and ▲同飛, aiming for both the pawn at 6四 and a ▲２三歩 drop. However, the sequence continues with ▽１四歩, ▲６四飛, ▽１三角, ▲４八玉, and the pawn drop ▽２八歩, leading to "Figure 2."

In "Figure 2," Sente is in a difficult position. If Sente captures the pawn with ▲同銀, Gote plays ▽２六歩. If Sente captures with ▲同飛, Gote plays ▽４六角. This illustrates why the early ▲１六歩 is vital—it prevents Gote from effectively utilizing the 1三 square for the Bishop in these counter-sequences.

Figure 2


Because the pawn drop ▽２八歩 results in the loss of the Knight, the "Cross-Rook" (Juuji-bisha) tactic fails, leaving Sente at a disadvantage. Gote's ▽１三角 move is specifically played to prevent Sente from returning the Rook to ▲２四飛 in response to ▽２八歩. If ▲１六歩 had been played prior to the situation in "Figure 2," Sente would have been able to escape with ▲１七桂. In response to ▲１六歩, Gote counters with ▽１四歩. If Gote does not defend this way, Sente can push further with ▲１五歩 to aim for a different strategy entirely, but we will cover that in a later chapter.

By playing ▽１四歩 early, Gote defends against the Cross-Rook's ▲２四歩 drop; after ▽同歩 and ▲同飛, Gote can defend with ▽６三銀 (since a ▲２三歩 drop can be met by escaping with ▽１三角).

If Gote chooses not to retreat their Rook even after Sente plays ▲７七桂, the sequence leading up to Figure 3 becomes possible. Sente uses the ▲２四歩 pawn sacrifice (the "matching pawn") which carries the threat of the aforementioned Cross-Rook tactic. The moment Gote defends with ▽６三銀, Sente pulls the Rook back one square to ▲２五飛, now aiming for ▲２四歩 followed by ▲２三歩成.

When Gote drops ▽２三歩 to defend against this, Sente plays ▲８五飛, utilizing the support of the Knight at ７七 to force a Rook exchange. The resulting position in Figure 3 is advantageous for Sente. Consequently, Gote is generally forced to retreat their Rook in response to ▲７七桂. We will look at the subsequent developments in the next chapter.
