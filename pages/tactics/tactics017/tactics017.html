Quick Ishida Strategy [2]

　

In the early stages of the Quick Ishida, Sente's formation appeared defenseless, but we saw that any attempt by the Static Rook side to strike early ended in failure. This time, we will introduce the offensive sequence that gives the "Quick Ishida" its name—where Sente proactively forces a rapid-attack scenario.

Figure 1 shows the position where Gote has played ▽6二銀 from the previous chapter's starting point to reinforce the defense of the 7th file. It is at this point that Sente launches the signature attacking sequence of the Quick Ishida strategy.

After ▲7四歩 ▽同歩, Sente exchanges Bishops and drops ▲5五角. Just as it looks like the fork on the Rook and Silver has decided the game, Gote brilliantly covers both threats simultaneously with ▽7三銀. However, Sente charges forward again with ▲7四飛.

The aim is that if Gote plays ▽同銀, Sente plays ▲8二角成, winning back the Rook and creating a Uma (Horse) to gain the advantage. In any case, it is a flashy sequence where the Rooks and Bishops fly all over the board.

Gote seemed to be in trouble at Figure 2, but the move ▽6四角 was available. If Gote had played ▽3三角 instead, Sente would respond with ▲7三角成 ▽同桂 ▲同飛成. Because Sente's King has already moved to ▲4八玉, Gote's counter-tactic of ▽9五角 (the King-Rook fork) does not work here.

Please be careful: when you adopt the Quick Ishida as Gote, there is a one-tempo difference; your King will still be at 5-1, meaning this King-Rook fork tactic remains a threat you must watch out for.

A key point leading up to Figure 3 is that when Sente plays ▲7三飛成, Gote takes the Bishop with ▽5五角. Sente cannot afford to let the Dragon escape, so the sequence continues from ▲8二龍 into a Rook exchange, resulting in Figure 3. Note that even if Gote had played ▽3三角 instead of ▽6四角, the result would be the same if Sente plays ▲7三飛成 to take the Silver, so the same position can arise for Gote as well.



If analyzed objectively, the position in Figure 3 is considered unclear (even). However, for beginners, it is quite difficult to find the best defensive responses required to navigate this sequence. Furthermore, many players would feel that Sente is easier to play in Figure 3 because they have successfully created a Ryu (Dragon). Therefore, it might be quite interesting to try this strategy out in an actual game.
