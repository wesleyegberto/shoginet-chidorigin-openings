Vertical Pawn Take (Tatefudori) [1]

From the Aigakari opening (Chapter 1), I will introduce the Tatefudori (Vertical Pawn Take) strategy, which aims to maximize Sente's opening advantage.

First, I will explain the case mentioned briefly in Chapter 1, where Sente initiates the ▲２四歩 pawn exchange on the 5th move instead of playing ▲７八金.

We saw the sequence up to the 14th move, ▽３五角打, in Figure 2 of Chapter 1. Following that, the Rook escapes, and Sente manages to recapture the Bishop via ▲２二歩成; however, by Figure 1, Gote has created a Horse, leaving Sente at a disadvantage.

If Sente plays ▲２三歩 instead of taking the pawn with ▲同歩 at the ▽８六歩 stage, they can capture the Bishop first. However, after ▽８七歩成, ▲２二歩成, and ▽同銀, Gote's Rook is not "floating" (unprotected), so Sente has no effective follow-up even with a Bishop in hand. Ultimately, Sente is forced to play ▲２八飛, allowing Gote to take back the Bishop with ▽８八と, leaving Sente simply one Pawn down.

In the sequence, if Gote takes the ▲２二歩成 with ▽同銀 instead of ▽同飛, Sente hits back with ▲３六角打. Gote's only response is ▽６二飛, but Sente can then turn the tables with the brilliant move ▲５二歩打.

I have highlighted this specifically in Figure 2; please take a moment to appreciate the brilliance of this Pawn drop.
Figure 2


　

If Gote takes this ▲５二歩 with ▽同飛, Sente responds with ▲６三角成. If Gote takes with any other piece or flees with ▽４二玉, the Rook's horizontal protection is cut off, and Sente plays ▲２二飛成, resulting in a winning advantage for Sente.

Based on the above, attempting ▲２四歩 on the 5th move in the Aigakari sequence is unreasonable; therefore, ▲７八金 is the correct move.

Targeting the Pawn on 3-4, which Gote advanced to open the Bishop's diagonal, by playing ▲３六飛 is called the Tatefudori (Vertical Pawn Take) strategy—as opposed to the Yokofudori (Side Pawn Pick) seen in Chapter 4 of the opening. We will continue from here in the next chapter.
