Fourth-file Rook – Combat [1]

There is a Shogi proverb that says, "Against Ranging Rook, aim for a Rapid Attack (Kyusen)."

Recently, Static Rook players have increased their use of endurance strategies (Jikyusen), such as the Ibisha Anaguma (Static Rook Bear-in-the-hole) introduced in Chapter 9, which focuses on solidifying the King. However, even today, the rapid attack remains a highly effective and significant countermeasure.

Targeting the head of the Bishop is a highly effective way to initiate a rapid attack against the Fourth-file Rook. In response, pre-emptively moving the Rook to the file where the opponent is likely to attack (▽3二飛) is a standard tactic for the Fourth-file Rook—and indeed for the Ranging Rook side in general.

The second stage of the counterattack is the ▽4五歩 strike, launched the very moment Sente sends the Silver forward with ▲4六銀 to reinforce the assault. After the Bishops are exchanged and Sente advances with ▲3五銀, the pawn drop at ▽3七歩 becomes the decisive blow that secures the advantage. Since Sente loses their Silver if they move the Rook away, they are forced to play ▲同飛. Following the sequence to "Figure 2," Gote has achieved a superior position, having gained material, promoted a Bishop into a Horse, and established a significant lead in the activity of the Ranging Rook.

Note that if Sente tries to exchange pawns with the Rook (▲同飛) after ▲3五歩 ▽同歩 instead of playing ▲4六銀, Gote has the excellent move ▽2二角. This forces a Rook exchange with ▲3二飛成 ▽同銀, leading to "Figure 3."

Figure 3

In this position as well, while the Fourth-file Rook side has absolutely no gaps for a Rook drop, the Static Rook side has many openings at squares like 2-7 or 2-8, meaning Gote is once again superior. It is a standard principle in rapid-attack scenarios that "a Rook exchange favors the Ranging Rook."
