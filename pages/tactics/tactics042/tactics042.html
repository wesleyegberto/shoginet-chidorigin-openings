The purpose of ▽７四飛 is to prevent Sente from pushing the pawns on the 3rd through 6th files, thereby restricting Sente's options. In response, Sente plays ▲７七角, aiming for a formation called "Ginkammuri" (Silver Crown) via ▲８八銀, ▲８六歩, and then ▲８七銀. Since allowing this would make the game difficult for Gote, Gote exchanges Bishops and attempts to suppress Sente with ▽３三桂 followed by ▽２五歩.

If the Rook retreats downward, Gote will capture the pawn with ▽７六飛, so Sente escapes with ▲６六飛. If Sente plays ▲５六飛 instead, Gote can target the position with ▽４五角, followed by ▽１五歩 ▲同歩 ▽１八歩. In Figure 2, Gote's attack is faster, and Sente is in a disadvantageous position.

In Figure 1, there is also the option to push ▲８六歩 and initiate action from the 8th file first. Since Gote's movements are difficult here, they swing the Rook with ▽８四飛 to target this pawn. However, when the Rook's horizontal reach is blocked by ▲７五歩 and ▽７四歩, Sente launches an attack with ▲１五歩.

If Gote responds with ▽同歩, Sente has the sequence ▲１二歩 ▽同香 ▲３三角成 ▽同桂 ▲２一角打, which is a fork on the Gold and Lance. Gote’s ▽２五歩 aims to ensure that if Sente plays ▲同飛, the ▽３三桂 move (after a Bishop exchange) will hit the Rook. Following the maneuvers through ▲１七桂 to develop the Knight, Figure 3 shows Sente in a superior position. The ▲２二歩 drop during the sequence is an exquisite move: if Gote takes with ▽同角, Sente wins immediately with ▲３二飛成.

We have looked at two examples of how the Aerial Battle Strategy (Kuchu-senpo) can progress from Figure 1. Neither of these examples represents an absolute set of "only moves," nor do they provide a final conclusion on the evaluation of the position. However, I believe they serve as excellent examples to help you understand the characteristic movements and tactical flow for both Sente and Gote in this strategy.
