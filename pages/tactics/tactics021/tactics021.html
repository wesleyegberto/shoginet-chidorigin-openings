Demon Killer (Oni-goroshi) Strategy [2]

The reason I mentioned at the end of the previous chapter that the standard "Demon Killer counter-measures" found in theory books are insufficient is because a certain irregular sequence exists.

Up until ▽6二銀, just before Figure 1, the sequence is identical to the Quick Ishida and the Masuda-style Ishida. After observing Gote play ▽6二銀, Sente then jumps the Knight with ▲7七桂.

Realizing that ▽6二銀 is a poor move against the Demon Killer, Gote hurriedly retreats the Silver and repositions with ▽6二金. However, losing two tempos in this manner proves fatal. Compared to Figure 2 in the previous chapter, Sente's Rook is already positioned on the 7th file and the pawn has reached 7-5, making Gote's defense too late. This is the consequence of simply memorizing the pattern "use ▽6二金 against the Demon Killer" without understanding the context.

There is no meaning in memorizing tactical patterns by rote; you must understand their underlying intent. When we consider the objective of the Demon Killer, it is to launch a rapid assault using the Rook, Bishop, and Knight, targeting the Rook at 8-2, the 7-3 square, and the opposite 2-2 or 1-1 squares.

Looking at Figure 1 again, Sente's offensive formation is certainly more complete than the standard Demon Killer. However, in terms of a "rapid attack," the timing is actually delayed. Once you realize this, the strategy becomes clear: punish Sente's delay and shut down their objectives. First, Gote plays ▽3二金. This neutralizes one of the primary targets. Then, when Sente jumps ▲6五桂, Gote plays ▽7二金 to reinforce the 7th file.

While playing ▽6四歩 instead of ▽7二金 would also allow Gote to take the Knight with ▽6五歩 (since the ▲5五角 follow-up after a Bishop exchange no longer creates a double attack), ▽7二金 is a more solid and safer choice in this position. When Sente plays ▲2二角成 ▽同銀 ▲4六角 to target the Rook side, Gote employs the strongest defensive principle—"Counter a Bishop with a Bishop"—by dropping ▽6四角, completely neutralizing the Demon Killer. Even if Sente follows with ▲同角 and another ▲4六角 drop, Gote responds with ▽6三金 reaching Figure 3, at which point Sente has completely run out of options.

In this sequence, Sente's ▲4六角 is superior to ▲5五角. This is because ▲5五角 fails to create a fork in this situation. Unless there is a specific advantage, it is generally better to use the Rook and Bishop to exert pressure from a distance.

As shown here, even if the formation varies, the Demon Killer can be defeated as long as you understand the opponent's objectives and move to defend against them one step ahead.
