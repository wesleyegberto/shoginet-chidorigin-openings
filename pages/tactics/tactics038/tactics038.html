Side Pawn Picker - ▽3八 Pawn Strategy [2]

In this chapter, we will explore the objectives and power of the ▽３八歩 variation. This strategy has few practical examples in competitive Yokofudori (Side Pawn Picker) games and can be considered a "hidden" or "underground" line of play.

In Figure 1, Gote is threatening to play ▽２八歩. To avoid this, Sente could consider moves such as ▲２四飛, ▲３九金, or ▲１六歩. However, regardless of which one is chosen, Gote will utilize the sunuki (uncontested capture) of the Rook as shown in Figure 2, leaving Gote in a favorable position. Playing ▲３六飛 to prevent this is also clearly disadvantageous, as ▽２八歩 would trap and kill the Knight. Sente lacks a way to defend against both threats simultaneously.

In truth, there is a problem with the sequence leading to Figure 1. In the previous chapter's progression, playing ▲８七歩 in response to ▽４四角 was not a mistake. If Sente had played ▲７七角 instead, they would have been in trouble after ▽同角成 followed by ▽８九角. The real issue was playing ▲７七銀 when Gote took the side pawn with ▽７六飛. While moving the Silver up is usually the standard, good-looking defensive shape, in this specific position, it was a blunder.

The correct move instead of ▲７七銀 is the ▲７七歩 drop. Following ▽７四飛, even if the Rook is captured via sunuki (▲２四飛 ▽７七角成 ▲同銀 ▽２四飛) as in Figure 2, Sente can play ▲１五角. After ▽２三歩 ▲２四角 ▽同歩, the Rook and Bishop are exchanged. Unlike the situation in Figure 2, there is no material loss, and the game remains even. This is shown in Figure 3.

As mentioned at the beginning, there are very few practical game examples of the ▽３八歩 strategy, and there are almost no game records available to serve as a reference. However, that is precisely why it can be considered so interesting. This is true not only for the ▽３八歩 strategy but for Yokofudori in general: if the opponent is unfamiliar with the lines, the success rate is remarkably high, making it possible to win even against an opponent with a somewhat higher skill level.

Particularly with this ▽３八歩 strategy, there is a strong possibility that your opponent will be seeing it for the first time, so why not give it a try?
