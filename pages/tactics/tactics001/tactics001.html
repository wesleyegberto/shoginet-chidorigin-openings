Double Wing Attack (Aigakari)

Using the Rook while keeping it in its original position is called Ibishu (Static Rook). An opening where both Sente and Gote use Static Rook is known as Ai-Ibishu (Double Static Rook). Within that category, a strategy where Sente plays ▲２六歩 as the first move to advance the Rook's pawn, and Gote similarly responds with ▽８四歩, is called Aigakari (Double Wing Attack). Furthermore, an opening that begins this way is referred to as "Aigakari-style Shogi."

In this sequence, why doesn't Sente immediately push ▲２四歩 on the 5th move instead of playing ▲７八金? This is a point explained in almost every introductory Shogi book, but since it cannot be overlooked, I will provide a brief explanation.
Figure 2
　

If Sente plays ▲２四歩 on the 5th move, the sequence continues: ▽同歩, ▲同飛. At that point, Gote pushes ▽８六歩. If Sente plays ▲同歩, Gote drops ▽８七歩. Following ▲２三歩打, ▽８八歩成, and ▲同銀, Gote plays ▽３五角打, using the Bishop captured earlier to target the floating Rook. This results in an unfavorable position for Sente. (Figure 2)

Because Sente has a Pawn in hand after playing ▲８六同歩, Gote is able to drop ▽８七歩. Therefore, Sente plays ▲７八金 to protect the head of the Bishop, and only after Gote responds with ▽３二金 does Sente push ▲２四歩.

After proceeding to Figure 1, the strategy branches out further depending on whether Sente retreats the Rook to ▲２八飛 or ▲２六飛.
