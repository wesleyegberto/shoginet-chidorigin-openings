Ranging Rook (Furibisha)

Up until now, we have introduced strategic formations where both Sente and Gote use the Rook in its original starting position. However, there is a method of fighting where the Rook is moved horizontally to a different location during the opening. This is called Furibisha (Ranging Rook).

Figure 1


Just as in the previous Yokofudori (Side Pawn Picker) example, Sente opens the Bishop's diagonal with ▲7六歩 and Gote responds by opening their own with ▽3四歩. However, when Gote follows Sente's ▲2六歩 with ▽4四歩 to close the Bishop's diagonal, it signals a Furibisha-moyo (Ranging Rook style) setup. While this move order is not an absolute guarantee, it makes a Ranging Rook strategy highly likely. The reason for this is that when playing Ranging Rook, one typically defends the 2nd file by moving the Bishop to ▽3三角 to block the opponent's Rook-file pawn exchange; closing the diagonal beforehand ensures that the Bishop cannot be exchanged during this maneuver.

In response to Gote's ▽3四歩, Sente chooses to close the Bishop's diagonal with ▲6六歩 on the third move, aiming for a Ranging Rook strategy. This results in the position shown in "Figure 2."

Sente can choose to play Ranging Rook even if Gote plays ▽8四歩 on the second move; similarly, Gote can play Ranging Rook even if Sente opens with ▲2六歩. Both "Figure 1" and "Figure 2" are representative of the tactical formation known as Shiken-bisha (Fourth-file Rook).
