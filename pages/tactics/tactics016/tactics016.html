Quick Ishida Strategy [1]

　

As mentioned in the explanation of Figure 1 in Chapter 9, the "Ishida Style Third-file Rook" is one of the ideal formations for Third-file Rook players. However, since the Static Rook side rarely allows this to be realized easily, the "Full Ishida" (Ishida-ryu Hon-gumi) is not seen very often.

The name "Ishida Style" originates from its creator, Ishida Kengyo, a blind Shogi master from the Edo period.

In this lesson, we will look at the "Haya-ishida" (Quick Ishida) strategy—the first in a series of tactics that fundamentally aims for the Ishida formation while harboring distinct offensive goals.

While the standard joseki for Ranging Rook is to avoid Bishop exchanges, the Quick Ishida (Haya-ishida) is characterized by boldly pushing the pawn to ▲7五歩 regardless of the consequences. At first glance, this formation appears to be full of gaps, and one might wonder if it is truly viable. Let’s examine how the variations unfold.

In Figure 1, the first move that comes to mind is pushing the Rook's pawn with ▽8六歩. However, the moment Gote plays ▲同歩 ▽同飛, Sente strikes with ▲7四歩. If Gote captures with ▽同歩, it leads to Figure 2: a devastating "eye-watering" fork on the King and Rook with ▲9五角, resulting in a win for Sente. If Gote does not take the ▲7四歩, Sente will break through the 7th file with ▲7三歩成. Furthermore, defending with ▽6二銀 is also futile; after a Bishop exchange, Sente can drop ▲7七角, forking the Rook and Silver to secure a winning advantage.

In Figure 1, it also seems natural for Gote to exchange Bishops and drop ▽4五角, aiming to promote at either 2-7 or 6-7. However, Sente has a counter-technique by dropping ▲7六角. If Gote proceeds with ▽2七角成, Sente also has the move ▲4三角成, creating a Uma (Horse) while threatening the Knight. Since this exchange of promotions favors Sente, Gote instead defends with ▽3二金. However, after Sente plays ▲3八玉, Gote's Bishop is unable to promote on either side, and the attempt ends in failure by Figure 3.



As we've seen, despite appearing vulnerable, Sente's camp does not collapse easily. In the next chapter, we will introduce the core attacking lines of the Quick Ishida, where Sente takes aggressive action against a more peaceful defensive setup from Gote.
