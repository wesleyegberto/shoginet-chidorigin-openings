Side Pawn Picker (Yokofudori) Strategy [1]

One of the most frequently played opening styles in the current professional Shogi world is the "Side Pawn Picker" (Yokofudori) strategy, which was introduced as Opening Pattern 4 in Chapter 4.
The "Vertical Pawn Picker" strategy discussed through Chapter 26 was named as a contrast to this Yokofudori. While the act of actually taking the pawn rarely occurs in the "Vertical" style, the Side Pawn Picker is an intense strategy that branches into numerous variations the moment the pawn is captured.

This move, ▲3四飛 in Figure 1, is the fundamental starting point of the Side Pawn Picker — a strategy that has existed since the Edo period. However, in the past, there was a saying: "Picking the side pawn leads to three years of trouble." It was believed that if Gote played ▽2三歩, the Rook should retreat to 2六 or 2八; taking the pawn at 3四 was considered a greedy, losing move.

The reason for this belief is that from Figure 1, Gote has the counter of ▽8八角成 ▲同銀 ▽2五角 — exchanging Bishops and dropping a new one to simultaneously target the Rook and the ４三 square. Note that dropping the Bishop at ▽4五角 instead of 2五 is a mistake that leads to a disadvantage for Gote. Let's look at what happens if Gote does play ▽4五角.

If the Bishop is dropped from ４五, Sente simply escapes with ▲３五飛. When Gote follows with ▽２七角成, Sente has the counter-drop of ▲１五角, putting the King in check. Although the King escapes and Sente opens an escape route with ▲３六歩 (making the initial Bishop drop appear meaningless), by Figure 2 where Sente plays ▲４八角 in response to ▽１四歩 — a move to "kill" Gote’s Horse with ▲２八歩 has been created. Even if Gote recognizes this, there is no way to prevent it. Ultimately, Gote is forced to sacrifice the piece with ▽４九馬.

That is the reality: the sequence starting from ▲１五角 appears wasteful at first glance, but it actually contains the terrifying objective of immobilizing and killing Gote’s Horse. Therefore, dropping the Bishop at ４五 is not a viable option, and ▽２五角 is the correct move.

In response to ▽２五角, Sente plays ▲３二飛成, decisively sacrificing the Rook for the Gold. Sente then plays ▲３八銀 to prevent the Bishop from promoting. Unless you are familiar with this established theory (joseki), it is a sequence that seems very hard to believe is favorable. For Gote's part, moving ▽３三銀 as shown in Figure 3 to prevent ▲２二角 is the basic form for this variation. We will continue from here in the next chapter.
