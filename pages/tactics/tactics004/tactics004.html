Side Pawn Picker (Yokofudori, 横歩取り)

　

As a basic opening formation for Ai-Ibisha (Double Static Rook), there is a setup known as Yokofudori-moyo (Side Pawn Picker style). This strategy becomes a possibility when Gote responds to Sente's ▲7六歩 by also opening their Bishop's diagonal with ▽3四歩, instead of playing ▽8四歩.

When Sente plays ▲2六歩 to signal their intent for a Static Rook game against Gote's second move ▽3四歩, and Gote responds with ▽8四歩, the game develops as shown in the diagram on the left. In this position, if Gote plays ▽2三歩, Sente can choose not to retreat the Rook but instead play ▲3四飛, capturing Gote's side pawn. This is the "Yokofudori" (Side Pawn Picker) strategy. Unlike in Aigakari, the fact that the Bishop's diagonal was opened means Gote's pawn at 3四 is "floating" (undefended), making it a prime target.

However, unlike in Aigakari, Gote's Bishop is not trapped even if Sente plays ▲2三歩 later. Therefore, it is currently more common for Gote to skip ▽2三歩 and instead exchange their own Rook-file pawn with ▽8六歩, ▲同歩, and ▽同飛.

In either case—whether Gote plays ▽2三歩 or exchanges pawns on the 8th file—the decision of whether or not to capture the side pawn is left to Sente. In actual play, capturing the pawn is more common, as it shows a refusal to be intimidated by the opponent's spirit.

The opening sequence up to the 4th move, ▽8四歩, is what we call Yokofudori-moyo (Side Pawn Picker style).
