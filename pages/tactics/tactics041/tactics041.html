Side Pawn Picker - ▽3三 Bishop "Aerial" Strategy (Kuchu-senpo) [1]

Yoko-fudori (Side Pawn Picker) is a popular strategy in the current professional Shogi world, often played on grand stages like the Meijin-sen. This is due to the emergence of new strategies that have refined the traditional ▽８五飛 variation. Moving forward, I would like to explain these developments in order, leading up to the ▽８五飛 strategy.

First, we will look at the foundational variation known as the "Aerial Battle Strategy" (Kuchu-senpo), originated by 9th Dan Kunio Naito, who is also famous as a singer.

Playing ▽３三角 in response to Sente capturing the side pawn is the fundamental move of the Aerial Battle Strategy (Kuchu-senpo). Other ways to prevent the upcoming ▲２二角成 include Gote initiating the Bishop exchange (as seen in the three strategies from previous chapters) or playing ▽３三桂, but ▽３三角 is the most frequently played option.

Following ▽３三角, Sente plays ▲３六飛 to protect the pawn at 7六 and maintain the material advantage of being one pawn up. When Gote plays ▽８四飛, Sente responds with ▲２六飛. If Sente were to play ▲８七歩 prematurely here, Gote would swing the Rook to ▽２四飛, forcing Sente into the disadvantageous position of "apologizing" with ▲２八歩. One might worry about Gote dropping a pawn at ▽８六歩 in response to ▲２六飛, but there is no need for concern: ▲２一飛成 ▽８七歩成 ▲３三角成 leads to a winning position for Sente.

Gote plays ▽５二玉. With this, the aforementioned line starting from ▲３三角成 is defended, as Gote can now respond to ▲２一角打 with the retreating move ▽３一金. Sente likewise plays ▲５八玉, and both sides proceed to build solid formations without openings. This type of formation, where the King is positioned on the second rank in the center, is called "Nakazumai" (Central Residence).

Gote's ▽７四飛 is a powerful move aimed at the 7六歩, similar to the Tate-fudori (Vertical Pawn Picker) style, serving to check Sente's movements. The battle from this point onward will be covered in the next chapter.
