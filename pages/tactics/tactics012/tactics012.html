Figure 3

At "Figure 3," the Gote King is in checkmate, and it is game set. Of course, this result assumes Gote responded straightforwardly to every move, so variations would likely occur before reaching this exact position. However, regardless of the variations, Sente holds the advantage from the moment the attack is initiated with ▲1五歩.

The key point here is capturing back with the Silver via ▲同銀 after ▽1五同歩. This edge attack is another major objective of the Climbing Silver (Bougin) strategy, and it is not limited only to Yagura.

Because this Climbing Silver attack exists if the edge is pushed too early, it is often safer not to respond to an edge pawn push. This is why there is a common Shogi proverb: "Do not push the edge pawn in a Yagura castle."
