Chapter 10 - Yagura Strategy – Combat [1]

Now, picking up from "Figure 2" of the chapter 10, let's watch as Sente utterly smashes Gote's solid Yagura castle to pieces in one fell swoop.
Figure 1

　

Figure 1 is the same position as "Figure 2" from the previous chapter 10. For the moves leading up to this point, please refer to that chapter. This offensive formation on Sente's side is one of the ideal attacking patterns against the Yagura, known as "Yagura-kuzushi" (Yagura Breaker).

Please also take a moment to confirm that Sente's overall formation satisfies all three major principles of piece development mentioned in chapter 9:

  1. Rook and King are on opposite sides: The King is safely tucked away in the 8-8 square while the Rook is active on the 4th file.
  2. Castle consists of three Gold/Silver pieces: The King is protected by two Golds and one Silver in a classic Yagura structure.
  3. Attacking with Rook, Bishop, Silver, and Knight: All four pieces are perfectly positioned to coordinate an assault on Gote's camp.

Upon reaching "Figure 2," Sente has achieved a decisive victory. An important point in the sequence is the tactical pawn drop ▲4四歩 after ▲4五歩 ▽同歩. If Sente were to simply play ▲同銀 here, Gote would respond with ▽4四歩; following that, even if Sente attacks with ▲同銀 ▽同銀 ▲同角 ▽同金 ▲同飛, Gote can defend with ▽4三銀, and the attack would not go as well as intended. This is shown as a failure example in "Figure 3."

Figure 3 　

To prevent Gote from defending with ▽4四歩, Sente plays ▲4五銀 followed by ▽同銀, then drops ▲4四歩 again. This forces ▽5三金, allowing Sente to play ▲4五桂, capturing the Silver while simultaneously attacking the Gold. In the resulting position shown in "Figure 2," Gote's camp is beyond saving.

Furthermore, the move ▲6一銀, which attacks both the Rook and Gold simultaneously, is a famous tactic known as "Wariuchi no Gin" (The Splitting Silver).

While there are other variations in the moves leading to "Figure 2," Sente remains victorious regardless of how Gote plays after ▲4五歩. This demonstrates just how ideal and powerful Sente's formation in "Figure 1" truly is.
