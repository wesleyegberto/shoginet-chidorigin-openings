New Demon Killer (Oni-goroshi) Strategy

Over the past two segments, I introduced the Demon Killer's trap sequences and explained how to defend against them. However, there is a variation known as the "New Demon Killer," a strategy devised by the Eternal Kisei, Yonenaga.

This is not a mere trap, but a legitimate strategy that stands on its own. Because of this, it is complex with many variations. To conclude the Demon Killer series, we will look at one example of its primary objective and how Gote can avoid it.

First, the Ishida-style strategies explained so far cannot be used unless Gote plays ▽3四歩 in response to ▲7六歩. This is because these techniques require the Bishop's diagonal to be open. If Gote plays ▽8四歩 on the second move and follows with ▽8五歩 against ▲7五歩, Sente is forced to play ▲7七角, much like a standard Ranging Rook opening.

If Sente then closes the diagonal with ▲6六歩 in response to ▽3四歩, it becomes a completely different type of game—one that is actually more common and seen more frequently in professional play. However, the New Demon Killer catches the opponent off guard by switching the Rook with ▲7八飛 here. Figure 1, reached after exchanging Bishops with ▽7七角成 and taking back with ▲同桂, is the basic formation of the New Demon Killer.

At first glance, this setup also appears full of holes. However, those who have followed this course should understand that attacking this formation carelessly is extremely dangerous. While many moves like ▽8六歩 are visible in Figure 1, let's look at the variation where Gote drops ▽5四角 as an example of what happens when Gote initiates an attack.

The moment Gote lets go of their Bishop, Sente drops ▲5五角. When Gote defends with ▽2二銀, Sente jumps the Knight to this side with ▲8五桂. Since Gote cannot capture with ▽同飛, they move the Silver up to 3三, but Sente plays ▲7三桂不成 (Knight non-promotion), leading to an advantage for Sente.

The key point is to move with non-promotion; if Sente plays ▲7三桂成 (promotion) here, Gote responds with ▽8七飛成, leaving the promoted Knight stranded and out of play, resulting in failure. Because Sente keeps a Bishop in hand, other types of attacks from Gote are similarly dangerous.

So, what about ▽6二金 at Figure 1? In that case, Sente plays ▲8八飛 and it becomes a different game entirely. However, outside of the specific context of the Demon Killer, ▽6二金 is a poor shape and not recommended. Because the Bishops have been exchanged and piece activation is easy, Sente can rearrange their formation, unlike in the standard Demon Killer. So, what should Gote do?

The simplest and safest countermeasure is to avoid the New Demon Killer by refusing to exchange Bishops, as shown up to Figure 3. If Sente tries to force the issue here with ▲2二角成 followed by ▲7七桂, the resulting position becomes similar to the countermeasures explained in the previous chapter. Additionally, delaying the opening of the Bishop's diagonal by moving the King to 3二 before playing ▽3四歩 is also an effective tactic for shutting down the New Demon Killer.

In those cases, as well as in Figure 3, Sente will likely choose to close the diagonal with ▲6六歩 and transition into a different strategy that follows the fundamentals of Ranging Rook. I hope to have another opportunity to explain those variations in the future.
