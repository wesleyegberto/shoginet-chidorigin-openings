In the previous chapters, we saw Gote move ▽３三角 after Sente took the side pawn with ▲３四飛. However, jumping the Knight with ▽３三桂 is also an option, and it serves as another powerful method for Gote.

If Sente returns with ▲２四飛 in response to ▽３三桂, it allows Gote to jump with ▽４五桂 and initiate a decisive battle. If Sente defends against ▽４五桂 with ▲４八銀, the position collapses after Gote plays ▽８八角成 followed by a ▽３三角 drop. Similarly, if Sente plays ▲５八玉, they will lose after a Bishop exchange followed by a ▽３五角 drop.

In this situation, Sente has the option of ▲２五飛, changing the Rook's position while simultaneously attacking the Knight. Following this, the sequence leads to Figure 2, where Sente sacrifices the Rook but is able to remove the promoted Knight with a ▲２四角 check. While the situation remains complex, Sente is considered slightly easier to play.

In the middle of the sequence, if Sente tries to defend with ▲５八歩 instead of ▲２二飛成 after ▽５七桂成, Gote plays ▽８八角成, ▲同銀, ▽同飛成; if Sente responds with ▲同金, it leads to a checkmate after ▽６八銀打. Therefore, playing ▽４五桂 immediately against Sente's ▲２四飛 is a bit overextended, and Gote would instead push ▽１四歩, leading to a profound and difficult game.

The progression to Figure 2 involves sequences that can be frightening for Sente, so another option in response to ▽３三桂 is to move ▲５八玉. This eliminates the possibility of the ▽４五桂 jump, leading to a calmer flow of the game. It should be noted that moving ▲４八玉 instead of ▲５八玉 over-commits Sente's formation and is considered a loss; in rapid-attack styles, ▲５八玉 offers wider versatility and often makes subsequent moves easier to manage.

The following is one example of how both sides might develop their pieces, but in any case, the ▽３三桂 strategy has few fixed joseki. Because the game becomes te-shogi (a game proceeding based on on-the-spot judgment), it becomes a difficult contest that tests each player's ability to formulate their own strategic vision.
