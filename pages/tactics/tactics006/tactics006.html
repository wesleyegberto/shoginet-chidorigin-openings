Double Ranging Rook (Ai-furibisha)

As the final opening formation, let's introduce Ai-furibisha (Double Ranging Rook), where both players move their Rooks horizontally.

Moves to Figure 1:

  • ▲6八飛

Figure 1


When Sente closes the Bishop's diagonal with ▲6六歩 aiming for a Ranging Rook, Gote responds by pushing the pawn at 3-4 further with ▽3五歩. This is a representative sequence for Ai-furibisha (Double Ranging Rook), where Gote also aims for a Ranging Rook strategy. There are many other variations, such as Gote playing ▽4四歩 to mirror Sente's formation, and both players have various choices for where to "swing" their Rooks.

Among Shogi strategies, this is an "unexplored territory" with relatively few established joseki (standard sequences). While there are not as many professional game records compared to other openings, it is a very interesting and dynamic way to play.
